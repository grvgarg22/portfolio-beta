Case Study: Pro-Fitness

Landing Pageâ€¢WebAppâ€¢Personasâ€¢Wireframesâ€¢User-Interfaceâ€¢Prototyping

Overview:

The objective of this case study was to enhance the user experience of an existing online furniture shopping platform. The goal was to make the process of buying furniture easy and intuitive for users, while also providing guidance according to their interior preferences. The project aimed to improve customer satisfaction, increase conversion rates, and establish the brand as a leader in the online furniture market.

Research and Analysis:

Conducted user interviews and surveys to understand pain points, motivations, and preferences of furniture shoppers. Analyzed competitors' websites and identified areas of improvement, such as navigation, product discovery, and checkout process. Utilized web analytics to identify user drop-off points, high bounce rates, and other areas of concern.

User Personas:

Persona 1:"Sophisticated Susan"
Demographics:

Female, aged 35-45, working professional, high income.

Psychographics:

Appreciates modern and luxurious furniture, values convenience and quality.

Goals:

Finding statement pieces for her upscale home, seeking personalized recommendations.

Persona 2:"Practical Paul"
Demographics:

Male, aged 25-35, young professional, moderate income.

Psychographics:

Values functionality and affordability, seeks furniture that optimizes space.

Goals:

Discovering versatile and space-saving furniture, comparing prices and customer reviews.

Information Architecture:

Designed a clear and intuitive information architecture for the landing page:
  • Hero section:

    Engaging headline, subheadline, and CTA button to convey the value proposition.

  • Product Categories:

    Categorized furniture sections by room, style, and type for easy browsing.

  • Filter and Sort:

    Implemented filters and sorting options for price, style, color, and customer ratings.

  • Product Details:

    Enhanced product pages with high-resolution images, detailed descriptions, and customer reviews.

  • Checkout Process:

    Streamlined and simplified the checkout process with a progress indicator and multiple payment options.

User Flow:

  • User sees an ad on Facebook for a work from home desk set-up.
  • User clicks on the ad and is directed to the landing page.
  • User navigates to the "Home Office" category and selects the "Desks" subcategory.
  • User filters the options by price range and selects a desk for more details.
  • User reads the product description, views images, and checks customer reviews.
  • User adds the desk to the cart and proceeds to the checkout.
  • User enters shipping and payment details, and completes the purchase.

Visual Design:

Developed five variations of color schemes to create an appealing visual experience that aligns with the brand identity and target audience's preferences.

Results and Impact:

Increased conversion rates by 15% within three months of the redesign. Improved user engagement metrics, such as decreased bounce rates and increased average session duration.

Positive feedback from users regarding the enhanced user experience and personalized product recommendations.

Strengthened brand perception as a reliable and customer-centric online furniture retailer.

Conclusion:

By conducting thorough research, analyzing user behavior, and implementing user-centered design principles, the online furniture shopping experience was significantly enhanced. The redesigned platform provided an intuitive and personalized experience, resulting in improved customer satisfaction, increased conversions, and a competitive advantage in the online furniture market. The project showcased the value of UX design in transforming a digital platform into a user-friendly and successful e-commerce destination.
